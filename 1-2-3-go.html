Investigate

Majors and Degree Options

Kent State offers more than 200 undergraduate and graduate majors and minors, connecting to a wide range of careers. Our academic advisors can put you on the degree track that will best help you reach your personal and career goals. Our Career Services Center also offers some helpful exercises than can help you choose a major and a career.

See Majors

Undecided? Explore!

If you’re not ready to declare a major yet, exploring our Engage section can help you find out more about campus life, culture and the resources available to you at Kent State. Then maybe you’ll have a better idea of what you’d like your major to be. After 45-credit hours, you will need to select a major so that you’ll be able to keep on track for graduation.

Student Accessibility

Kent State has been recognized for providing high-level support to students with disabilities through our support, assistive technology, scholarships and more. Our goal is to provide all students with equal opportunities, so that everyone can benefit, contribute and participate from all university programs, services and activities.

Explore Accessibility Services

You can also explore Kent State’s living learning communities - residence halls that group students with similar majors together, with access to resources and a faculty advisor for better collaboration on projects and opportunities for group study.

Engage with Kent State

Whether you are researching how to become a student at Kent State, waiting to hear the status of your application or have been recently admitted as a Golden Flash, the following list is designed to help give you a sense of how you will get started at Kent State.

Newly Admitted Students

You will receive an account to Kent State’s student portal, FlashLine. Once you log into your FlashLine account, you will gain access to a detailed, dynamic checklist. This checklist will act as your official go-to-guide to help you navigate your transition to the university.

New Freshman

Review the New Freshman Welcome Packet for detailed information on your next steps. Parents can review the Parent Welcome Packet for information to help keep their student on track.

Transfer, Post-Undergraduate and Guest Students

Review the Transfer, Post-Undergraduate and Guest Welcome Packet for detailed information on enrollment and registration information.

Your Finances

Enrollment Fees

Pay your enrollment fee to preserve your enrollment at Kent State. You can pay your fee online by logging into FlashLine or by mailing a check to Admissions.

Make a Payment

Financial Aid

Apply for financial aid as soon as possible after Jan. 1, and find scholarships. The deadline for scholarships is Jan. 15.

Get Financial Aid

FLASHcard

This is your student photo ID, your dining plan card and a reloadable debit card – all in one.

Get Your FLASHcard

Explore Payment Options

Visit the Bursar’s Office to see the fee schedule and find the method of payment that works best for you.

Before You Arrive

Apply for Housing, Along with a Dining Plan, or Request a Commuter Exemption

Kent State requires that students live on campus and participate in a dining plan for the first two years of enrollment. You can find out about residence hall options and theme living communities. Commuters can check out our special resources for commuter students.

Get Housing

Prior to arriving at Destination Kent State (DKS) you will be required to complete a placement assessment that is designed to help your academic advisor determine which courses will provide the best opportunity for your success. Your assessment areas will be available on FlashLine under the New@KSU Tab.

Campus Organizations and Activities

Get REC’d

Kent State’s state-of-the-art Student Recreation and Wellness Center (SRWC) is the place to go for swimming, exercise equipment, free weights, racquetball, basketball and more. In the spring, the SRWC also offers canoes and kayaks for Cuyahoga River excursions. Quick Tip: Avoid waiting in line for Group X classes and know if a class is full with Flash Pass.

Kent State offers a Zipcar car-sharing service on campus. For an affordable rate, students 18 and older can reserve a car to use for convenient, reliable transportation.

Borrow a bike

Flashfleet is the easy, convenient and free service that lets you pick up a bicycle to use for the day from a number of locations on campus.

Kent is located just a short drive from Cleveland, Akron, Youngstown and Canton. These major cities offer plenty to see and do with access to concert venues, entertainment districts, world-class museums, parks and zoos, and professional sporting events.
